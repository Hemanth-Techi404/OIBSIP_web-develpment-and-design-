About The Hitman

Rohit Gurunath Sharma, born on April 30, 1987, in Nagpur, Maharashtra, is one of the most prolific batsmen in international cricket. His journey from a promising young talent to the captain of the Indian cricket team is nothing short of inspirational.

What makes Rohit special is his ability to play long innings with effortless grace. His lazy elegance at the crease combined with explosive power makes him a nightmare for bowlers. The way he times the ball is often described as poetry in motion.

Beyond his cricketing skills, Rohit is admired for his calm demeanor, leadership qualities, and ability to perform under pressure. His transformation from a middle-order batsman to one of the greatest openers in ODI history is a testament to his adaptability and work ethic.

Career Milestones

International Debut (2007)

Made his international debut against Ireland in Belfast. Showed glimpses of his talent early in his career with a composed 50 in just his second ODI.

T20 World Cup Winner (2007)

Played a crucial role in India's inaugural T20 World Cup triumph in South Africa, including a memorable 30* in the final against Pakistan.

Transformation to Opener (2013)

Promoted to open the innings in ODIs during the Champions Trophy. This decision changed his career as he went on to become one of the greatest ODI openers.

First ODI Double Century (2013)

Scored 209 against Australia in Bangalore, becoming only the third Indian to score an ODI double century. This was just the beginning of his record-breaking spree.

World Record 264 (2014)

Smashed 264 against Sri Lanka at Eden Gardens - the highest individual score in ODI history. This innings showcased his incredible ability to accelerate.

Captaincy & 5 IPL Titles (2017-2023)

Took over Mumbai Indians captaincy and led them to record 5 IPL titles. Also became India's full-time white-ball captain, leading the team to numerous victories.
